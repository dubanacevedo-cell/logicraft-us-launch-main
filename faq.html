We design and build mission-critical transactional platforms — payment processing engines, logistics orchestration systems, financial data pipelines, and infrastructure where correctness and availability are non-negotiable. If your system touches money, inventory, or regulated workflows at scale, it is the kind of problem we exist to solve.
Every engagement begins with a discovery and architecture phase — typically one to two weeks — where we map your existing landscape, define the technical blueprint, and agree on milestones. From there, we work in structured sprints with weekly syncs, full backlog visibility, and documented delivery gates at each phase. Nothing moves forward without alignment.

We have built foundational infrastructure for early-stage companies that needed production-grade systems from the first line of code, and we have modernized legacy platforms for large financial institutions. What matters is the quality and complexity of the engineering problem — not the size of the organization.
Yes, frequently. We operate in embedded-team mode when in-house engineers are already in place, adapting to your existing workflows, code standards, and communication cadence. We can also lead a full turnkey engagement if you need an assembled team without an existing internal group.
Security is embedded in our engineering process from the first commit — not added at the end. We work regularly with SOC 2, PCI-DSS, and HIPAA-adjacent requirements. All deliverables go through internal security review before handoff, and we produce documentation suited for audit trails and compliance reporting as a standard deliverable.
A focused build — discovery through initial production deployment — typically runs 12 to 20 weeks for a core system. Larger programs with multiple subsystems, regulatory scope, or legacy migration complexity often extend to six to twelve months. We scope timing precisely during the discovery phase; we do not estimate before we understand the architecture.
Pricing depends on the shape of the engagement. Fixed-scope projects are quoted as a flat fee based on the architecture defined during discovery. Ongoing retainer and embedded-team engagements are structured on a time-and-materials basis with monthly billing. We discuss both models in the initial conversation — there is no single structure that fits every program.
We offer retainer-based support covering monitoring, incident response, and iterative feature development. If your goal is an eventual full handoff to an in-house team, we can structure a knowledge transfer and documentation package as part of the engagement close — including runbooks, architecture decision records, and onboarding sessions.
We do not take on volume work. Every engagement is staffed by senior engineers who have built and operated systems at scale — not junior developers following a template. Our focus is narrow by design: transactional, high-availability infrastructure. That specialization shows up in production metrics, SLA reports, and incident logs — not in slide decks.
